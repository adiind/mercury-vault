# Ken Nakagaki (中垣 拳)

**Assistant Professor**, Department of Computer Science, University of Chicago
**Lab**: Actuated Experience Lab (AxLab) — [axlab.cs.uchicago.edu](https://axlab.cs.uchicago.edu/)
**Email**: knakagaki@uchicago.edu
**Office**: JCL358 (Lab: JCL380), John Crerar Library

## Bio

Japanese interaction designer and HCI researcher. Founded AxLab at UChicago in 2022. His research focuses on **Actuated Tangible User Interfaces (A-TUIs)** — interfaces that physically actuate (shape-change and move) for novel user interaction. Passionate about creating physical embodied experiences through curiosity-driven tangible prototyping.

**PhD**: MIT Media Lab (Tangible Media Group), advised by Prof. Hiroshi Ishii
**Before UChicago**: Master's in Media and Governance (Interaction Design), Keio University SFC, Japan

## Research Focus

- **Actuated Tangible User Interfaces (A-TUIs)** — physically shape-changing and moving interfaces
- **Fluidic mechanisms** — interactive materials using fluidic channels
- **Shape-changing interfaces** — scissor mechanisms, linear/curve actuation tables
- **Self-propelled TUIs** — mobile robots on ceilings, tables, overhangs
- **Embodied interaction** — haptic experiences, force feedback, calligraphy learning
- **Everyday computing** — weaving digital interactions into physical tools and materials
